## User Personas 
### Persona 1: Maya — The Group Chat Reactor

**Archetype:** Fast, social, low-effort meme sender  
**Primary platforms:** Messages, Instagram, Discord  
**Goal:** Send a funny meme response as quickly as she would send a GIF.

Maya wants memes to feel instant. She usually sends reactions in group chats and does not want to open a full editing tool just to make one joke. For her, the meme generator needs to be fast, mobile-friendly, and easy to share directly into conversations.

**Behaviors**
- Gets most memes from Instagram, TikTok, friends, and group chats.
- Uses memes as quick reactions during conversations.
- Prefers tools that are already integrated into messaging apps.
- Often knows the emotion she wants to convey, but not the exact meme template.

**Pain Points**
- Existing meme generators feel too slow compared to GIFs.
- Searching for the right meme template is difficult.
- Meme websites are not optimized for quick mobile use.
- Too many editing steps make the joke feel less worth sending.

**Needs**
- Search by mood, scenario, or reaction.
- One-tap caption editing.
- Fast sharing to Messages, Instagram, and Discord.
- A workflow that feels as quick as sending a GIF.

### Persona 2: Leo — The Instagram Story Remixer

**Archetype:** Creative but impatient social poster  
**Primary platforms:** Instagram Stories, TikTok, CapCut, editing apps  
**Goal:** Remix existing memes into story-ready content without spending too much time editing.

Leo likes customizing memes for specific situations, friends, or inside jokes. He is willing to put in some effort, but current tools make the process feel unnecessarily complicated. He wants to take an existing meme image, GIF, or video and quickly change the text or format it for social media.

**Behaviors**
- Finds memes through social media.
- Uses Instagram Stories or TikTok when making or posting memes.
- Sometimes downloads memes and manually adds text.
- May use editing apps, but only because there is no easier option.

**Pain Points**
- Instagram text tools are limited and annoying to use.
- Editing apps like CapCut feel too complicated for simple meme edits.
- Video memes take too long to subtitle or format.
- Switching between multiple apps slows down the creative process.

**Needs**
- Automatic text replacement on existing memes.
- Story-sized export options.
- Simple video or GIF meme editing.
- AI help for placing captions correctly without manual layout work.

---

### Persona 3: Samir — The Template Hunter

**Archetype:** Has the joke, struggles to find the format  
**Primary platforms:** Google, meme generator websites, social media, group chats  
**Goal:** Find the right meme template and customize it without needing to know its official name.

Samir usually starts with a joke idea or a vague memory of a meme he has seen before. His biggest problem is not writing the caption, but finding the right format. He wants to describe what he is looking for in normal language and have the generator suggest relevant templates.

**Behaviors**
- Searches Google or meme websites when he wants a specific format.
- Remembers memes by visual description rather than by name.
- Starts from existing templates more often than blank images.
- Wants memes that feel current and not overused.

**Pain Points**
- Meme generators expect users to know template names.
- Browsing “top memes” does not help him find the right emotional tone.
- Existing templates can feel outdated or repetitive.
- AI-generated captions are not useful if the meme format is wrong.

**Needs**
- Natural-language meme template search.
- Trending meme browsing organized by mood or situation.
- Caption suggestions that match the selected template.
- Multiple template previews for the same joke idea.
